The Problem

Airtable was never built for a barn.

📋
One-Way Forms
The current Airtable setup is a one-way street. Fat-finger a number and you can't fix it — someone has to delete the record and re-enter everything from scratch.
🚫
No Validation
Bad data goes straight in. Eggs laid greater than hens alive. Mortality spikes that nobody flagged. Corrupted numbers that skew every report downstream.
📊
No Real Dashboard
Airtable doesn't roll up across houses. You can't open one screen and see how the whole ranch performed yesterday — per house, all metrics, at a glance.
"We're using it on some houses, not all. And when someone enters something wrong, we can't fix it."
— Discovery call. This is a solved problem.
The Solution

Log It.
Fix It. See It.
All in One Place.

A web app purpose-built for the Mahrts' operation — designed to run on an iPhone in the barn, validate data before it saves, and give the owner one screen every morning that shows exactly how the ranch is performing.

✏️
Editable Entries
Any worker can look up a previous day and correct a mistake. No re-entry. No asking someone to delete the bad record. Just fix it.
🛡️
Smart Validation
The app flags statistically unreasonable numbers before saving — eggs > hens alive, single-day mortality spikes, water consumption out of range. Bad data doesn't get in.
🏠
Multi-House Rollup
Every house tracked independently. One dashboard shows the whole ranch — per-house performance and ranch-wide totals, side by side, every morning.

How It Works

Workers log. The app calculates. The owner sees the full picture.

📱
Step 1 — Log
Barn worker opens the app on an iPhone. Enters the day's numbers per house — eggs, hen/rooster counts, deaths, water gallons. Validation runs before save.
⚙️
Step 2 — Calculate
App auto-computes Hen Day % (HDEP), rolling mortality rate, and feed efficiency (lbs per 100 birds per day, smoothed over last 2–3 bin cycles).
📊
Step 3 — Dashboard
Owner opens one screen. Sees every house's performance today vs. 7-day average. Red flags surface automatically. Any anomaly is visible before the day starts.

Daily Husbandry Log

Per house, per day. Entered on an iPhone in the barn. Exactly what the owner specified.

🥚
What Workers Log
Hen count (alive) · Rooster count (alive)
Hen deaths · Rooster deaths
Eggs laid · Gallons of water consumed

Tracked per house, every day.
🧮
What the App Calculates
Hen Day % (HDEP) = eggs ÷ hens alive × 100
Industry benchmark: 85%+ is healthy

Running mortality rate — cumulative + 7-day rolling per house
"Any worker can look up a previous day's entry and correct a mistake — without re-entering everything and asking someone to delete the bad record."
— Owner's specific pain point with Airtable. Fixed in Phase 1.

Feed Bin Tracker

Feed is the single biggest operating cost. This makes it visible.

🌾
What Workers Log
Bin opened: timestamp + lbs of feed added
Bin depleted: timestamp when bin empties

Each house has at least 2 bins. All tracked independently.
📐
What the App Calculates
Days to consume each bin load
Lbs of feed per 100 birds per day
Averaged across last 2–3 bin cycles to smooth imprecise recording

Displayed per house — anomalies are immediately visible
"A house suddenly consuming 15% more feed per 100 birds is an early warning sign — disease, equipment failure, or flock stress — before it shows up in egg counts."
— Feed efficiency surfaced early saves money and flock health.
